Last week Andrew brought in a very cheap solution to virtual reality hardware.  This was the Google Cardboard, which was introduced to Android developers in 2014.  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Cardboard  A new version (Cardboard 2) has been released in 2015 which is slighly larger and has a different input method.

It is simply a large piece of shaped cardboard, a couple of 45 mm focal length lenses, some velcro, a couple of magnets and a mobile phone with some software.  https://www.google.com/get/cardboard/

 

If you can get the lenses, you can build one yourself really easily; but to buy one from Amazon costs in the region of Â£6 - so just buy it.  http://www.amazon.co.uk/Cardboard-Virtual-Reality-Glasses-Headband/dp/B00V7Z2XM0

 

More excitingly for GCC is that you can download a 'unitypackage' for unity and create your own virtual worlds which you can experience on your Google Cardboard.  https://developers.google.com/cardboard/unity/

 

Finally, although this works on iOS and Android, only Android allows you to put the resulting program on the Android phone for free.  http://developer.android.com/develop/index.html  Until XCode 7 and iOS9 is released, you will need to pay Apple for a developer account to put your program on an iPhone.   https://developer.apple.com/programs/
